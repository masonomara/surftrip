## Email Templates for Personal Injury Firms:
# **Gathering Case** **Information**


Email Templates for Personal Injury Firms: Gathering Case Information

##### Gathering the right information can help you build a strong personal injury case. Having well-crafted email templates can make the process smoother and more efficient. These templates help you request key details from clients, such as digital evidence, insurance information, and personal statements while maintaining clear, professional, and supportive communication.

###### Each template is designed to guide clients through the process of working with your law firm, ensuring they understand what’s needed, why it matters, and how to provide the necessary information. By using these templates, you can streamline your workflow, keep cases moving forward, and reinforce trust with your clients.



_Tip:_



To make the best use of the templates in this guide, make sure to review
them and tailor them based on the services your firm provides. Based on the
situation, you may also want to combine messaging from multiple templates
into a separate communication.


Remember that these templates are provided for educational purposes only.
Lawyers should always use professional judgment when using or revising these
templates, which may require amendments based on your jurisdiction and the
needs of your particular file.

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #1**
### **Requesting a client’s authorization** **for investigative activities**

When handling your personal injury case, your client’s authorization is essential
to a thorough evaluation of their case. This email requests consent to proceed with
key investigative steps, such as medical records retrieval, expert witness interviews,
or other relevant activities.

For this, you will want to emphasize the importance of these investigative efforts,
demonstrate your commitment to handling the case with diligence and transparency,
and reassure the client that their consent is necessary to move forward effectively.

Use this template to streamline the authorization process and ensure all necessary
investigative actions can proceed without delay.


Subject: Authorization request for investigative actions


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


I hope you’re doing well. As we move forward with your personal injury case, there are certain
investigative activities that require your express consent to proceed. These may include

[BRIEFLY DESCRIBE ACTIVITIES, E.G., MEDICAL RECORDS RETRIEVAL, EXPERT WITNESS
INTERVIEWS, SURVEILLANCE, ETC.].


Attached, you will find a consent form that outlines these activities in detail. Please review
and sign it to authorize us to proceed with these necessary steps.


As these investigations can take time, we encourage you to get these consents filled as soon as
possible so that we can get started with this process.


We understand that this can be a difficult time. If you have any questions or would like
further clarification on any of the activities listed, don’t hesitate to reach out.


Thank you for your cooperation.


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #2**
### **Request for insurance information**

For your personal injury case, detailed insurance information is critical for pursuing
all potential avenues for compensation. Use this email to outline key insurance details
necessary to move forward with the case.

In this email, you will want to emphasize the importance of providing complete and
accurate insurance information, demonstrate your commitment to thoroughly assessing
the client’s options, and reassure them that you are prepared to navigate the complexities
of the claims process.

Use this template to quickly get the information you need.


Subject: Request for insurance information


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


In order to move forward with your personal injury case, we need to gather some key
information related to the insurance coverage involved in the incident.


Specifically, we would appreciate it if you could provide the following details:


1. Your insurance provider and policy number


2. The insurance provider of the at-fault party (if available)


3. Any claims or communications you’ve had with the insurance companies to date


This information will help us in assessing your case and ensuring we pursue all potential
avenues for compensation.


Your insurance provider should be able to provide this information to you, but if you need
assistance, please let us know.


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #3**
### **Request for your statement**

Presenting your client’s case accurately starts with understanding their accident
and its impact.

By using this email, you will want to provide clear guidance on what the statement
should include, reassure the client that assistance is available if needed, and ensure
they understand the importance of their input in the legal process.

Use this template to maintain a professional, supportive tone, encouraging the client
to provide a detailed, honest account while offering help if necessary.


Subject: Request for your statement


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


As part of our ongoing work to strengthen your personal injury case, we would like to request
a formal statement from you regarding the events surrounding the accident and its impact on
your life. This statement will help us accurately present the details of your case to the court or
opposing parties.


            - How the accident occurred


             - Your injuries and their impact on your daily life


             - Any emotional or psychological effects experienced


Feel free to write this statement in your own words, or if you would like assistance, we are
happy to guide you through the process. Please let us know if you have any questions or would
like to schedule a meeting to discuss this further.


Once we receive your statement, we will follow up to help ensure that your submission is an
accurate reflection of your experience.


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #4**
### **Document and evidence** **submission reminder**

Timely submission of documents and evidence is crucial to advancing your case.
With this communication, you will want to clearly list the documents or evidence
needed, offer assistance if they have questions, and emphasize the importance of
prompt submission.

This email serves as a friendly reminder, requesting the client to submit the necessary
materials to keep the process moving forward.

Use this template to remind the client of their responsibilities in the case while maintaining
a supportive tone and ensuring the client knows they can reach out for help if needed.


Subject: Reminder: Submit documents and evidence


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


We are still waiting on some important documents and evidence for your personal
injury case. To keep things moving forward, please send the following items at your
earliest convenience:


1. [LIST SPECIFIC DOCUMENTS OR EVIDENCE NEEDED, E.G., MEDICAL BILLS,

WITNESS STATEMENTS, ACCIDENT REPORTS, ETC.]


2. [ANY SPECIFIC FORMS OR SIGNED RELEASES, IF APPLICABLE]

If you have any questions about what is required or if you need help gathering these materials,
don’t hesitate to reach out. We want to ensure everything is in order so we can proceed with
your case as quickly as possible.


Thank you for your attention to this matter.


Best regards,
