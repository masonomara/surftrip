## Email Templates for Personal Injury Firms:
# **Updates on** **Case Progress**


Email Templates for Personal Injury Firms: Updates on Case Progress

##### Keeping your clients informed and engaged throughout their personal injury case helps build trust with the client, updates them on the status of their case, and reaffirms your firm’s commitment to supporting them throughout the journey.

###### These email templates help you communicate key updates, reminders, and requests in a professional yet approachable manner, making it easier for clients to stay informed and involved. From providing case updates and scheduling depositions to reminding clients about court dates and settlement negotiations, these templates streamline your communication while reinforcing your commitment to their case. Use them to maintain transparency, keep clients reassured, and encourage open dialogue every step of the way.



_Tip:_



To make the best use of the templates in this guide, make sure to review
them and tailor them based on the services your firm provides. Based on the
situation, you may also want to combine messaging from multiple templates
into a separate communication.


Remember that these templates are provided for educational purposes only.
Lawyers should always use professional judgment when using or revising these
templates, which may require amendments based on your jurisdiction and the
needs of your particular file.

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #1**
### **Case update**

When clients first reach out, you’ll want to follow up with a personal note and an
invitation to book a consultation. With this communication, you will want to
demonstrate that you’re able to look more closely at their situation, that you are
prepared to deal with a range of scenarios related to their problem, and that you
understand their cares and concerns.

Use this template to establish a personal connection with the client, showcase your
law firm’s expertise, and invite conversation to discuss the specifics of the client’s
situation. You can also use this email as a follow-up for clients that have submitted
any initial pre-screening information.

Email Templates for Personal Injury Firms: Updates on Case Progress


Subject: Update on your personal injury case


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to provide you with an update on the progress of
your personal injury case.


Since our last communication, our legal team has been diligently working on your behalf.
We have conducted thorough investigations, reviewed relevant documentation, and consulted
with experts in our pursuit of a successful resolution. We are committed to fighting for your
rights and ensuring that you receive the compensation you deserve.


As we move forward, we anticipate the following milestones in your case:


             - Discovery phase: We will continue gathering evidence, conducting depositions, and

exchanging information with the opposing party as we build a strong case on your

behalf.


            - Independent medical examinations: The defendant may request that you attend one
or more independent medical examinations (IMEs) for an “objective” evaluation of your
injuries. We may also arrange for IMEs if they are needed to help build your case.


             - Negotiation or mediation: Our team will engage in negotiations with the opposing party
in an effort to reach a fair settlement. If necessary, we will explore mediation options as a
means of resolving the matter amicably.


             - Trial preparation: In the event that a settlement cannot be reached, we will thoroughly
prepare for trial. This includes strategizing, finalizing witness testimonies, and ensuring
all necessary documentation and evidence are in order.


We understand that open and consistent communication is essential throughout this process.
We are here to address any concerns or questions you may have. Please do not hesitate to
reach out to us at [CONTACT INFORMATION] if you need any further information or if there
are any developments on your end that we should be aware of.


As always, your satisfaction and well-being are our top priorities. Our team is committed to
keeping you informed and supporting you every step of the way.


Thank you for entrusting your personal injury case to [LAW FIRM NAME]. We greatly
appreciate the trust you have placed in us.


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #2**
### **Update on case progress** **(short version)**

If you keep your client up to date on their case, you don’t always need to provide a
comprehensive overview of your approach to their case. With this template, you can
give your clients a quick, easy to read summary of the latest actions taken, recent
developments, and upcoming steps in the case. As well as a point of contact should
they have any questions or need more details.

Use this template to maintain transparency, demonstrate progress, and encourage
open communication between the client and your legal team.


Subject: Update on your personal injury case


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


We wanted to check in and provide you with a quick update on the progress of your case.
Here’s a brief overview of what’s happening:


           - [ACTION TAKEN, E.G., “WE HAVE SUBMITTED THE CLAIM TO THE INSURANCE

COMPANY.”]


           - [RECENT DEVELOPMENTS, E.G., “WE HAVE RECEIVED YOUR MEDICAL RECORDS

AND ARE REVIEWING THEM.”]


           - [UPCOMING STEPS, E.G., “NEXT, WE WILL SCHEDULE A DEPOSITION WITH

THE DEFENDANT.”]


We will continue to keep you updated and are always here to answer any questions you may
have. If you would like more details or have concerns, please don’t hesitate to get in touch.


Best regards,

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #3**
### **Court date reminder**

Reminders about important court dates are essential for ensuring your clients are prepared.
This email is a reminder of the upcoming court date. In your reminder, you will want to
highlight the court hearing’s date, time, and location while ensuring they know to be fully
prepared and offer assistance if needed.

Use this template to ensure the client feels supported and has the necessary information
to attend the court date.


Subject: Reminder: Court Date for [CLIENT NAME]’s personal injury case


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


This is a reminder that your court date for your personal injury case is scheduled for [DATE
AND TIME]. Please ensure that you arrive at [LOCATION] at least 30 minutes early so that we
can review our discussion on what to expect.


If you need any assistance or have any questions before the court date, please reach out to us.


We look forward to supporting you through this next step.


Best regards,

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #4**
### **Deposition or interview scheduling**

When scheduling a deposition, clear communication and flexibility are important to
ensuring your client is prepared and available. This email requests your client’s availability
for the deposition, offering multiple date options. In your correspondence, you will want
to be concise, provide clear options, and emphasize the next steps to ensure the client
understands what is required.

Use this template to make scheduling as easy as possible for the client while ensuring
they know what to expect moving forward.


Subject: Scheduling your deposition


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


We are in the process of scheduling your deposition for the personal injury case. Please let
us know your availability for the following dates:


                    - [DATE OPTION 1]

                    - [DATE OPTION 2]

                    - [DATE OPTION 3]


Once we receive your preferred date, we will confirm the details and provide further
instructions on what to expect during the deposition.


Thank you for your prompt response.


Best regards,

###### **EMAIL TEMPLATE #5**
### **Litigation notice to client**

Receiving a settlement offer is a key moment in your case, and it’s important to
communicate this with clarity and support. This email informs the client about
the settlement offer and encourages them to review the terms carefully.

You will want to explain the offer, advise the client on the next steps, and offer further
guidance if needed. Use this template to ensure the client feels informed and supported
as they make an important decision about their case.


Subject: Settlement offer for your personal injury case

We are pleased to inform you that we have received a settlement offer from the opposing
party regarding your personal injury case. Please reach out to our office to discuss the offer as
soon as possible.


During our call, I can share the offer, and provide recommendations for how to proceed. We
are here to guide you through this process and ensure you understand all available options.
