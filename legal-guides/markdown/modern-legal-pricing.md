The billable hour has been the backbone of legal pricing for decades. In
theory, it’s simple: track your time, multiply by your rate, and send the
invoice. In practice, the model increasingly clashes with modern client
expectations, competitive pressures, and operational realities.

While familiar and entrenched, the billable hour often creates friction
between lawyers and clients, and even within law firms. Many firms are
beginning to see that this approach is no longer the safest or most
profitable default.

### **Understanding Alternative** **Fee Arrangements (AFAs)**


Moving away from hourly billing doesn’t mean losing structure. It means
replacing a time-based system with one that is value-based and outcomefocused. AFAs provide predictable pricing for clients, fair compensation for
firms, and better alignment of interests.

Clients are pushing for greater transparency, faster timelines, and cost
certainty. At the same time, efficiency gains from technology and process
improvement make hourly pricing less viable. If efficiency keeps improving
but billing remains hourly, firms either lose revenue or resort to unrealistic
hourly rates that don’t reflect the value provided.

Industry data shows:

                              - Flat-fee work has grown by **34%** in the past year.

                              - **71%** of clients prefer paying a flat fee.

                              - Firms using flat fees are almost **twice as likely** to collect payment
immediately and **five times more likely** to send invoices promptly.

WHY AFAs WORK

**1. For Clients:** predictable costs, reduced billing anxiety, and
aligned incentives.

**2. For Legal Professionals:** reduce burnout, valued for more than
their time.

**3. For Firms:** improved and more predictable cash flow, reward
for efficiency, and competitive differentiation.

**4. For All:** shared focus on efficient, high-quality outcomes.


MAKING AFAs REPEATABLE

**1. Guidelines & Base Fees:** Define minimum fees for each service.

**2. Pricing Factors:** Use checklists to fully consider and adjust for
complexity and cost.

**3. Modifiers:** Apply value-based adjustments for considerations like
urgency, stakes, or internal strategic factors.

**4. Clear Service Descriptions:** State inclusions and exclusions to
prevent scope creep.

**5. Review & Learn:** Compare flat fees to recorded time, track
acceptance rates, and use learnings to refine pricing.

**AI = Efficiency → Efficiency = Less Time → Less Time ≠ Hourly Growth**

Document automation, AI-assisted research, and workflow

Without changing pricing models, each efficiency gain widens

Record time even on flat-fee matters. Compare it to the actual fee, and

### **The Modern Pricing** **Framework**


Switching away from the billable hour isn’t just about picking a new fee
model. It’s about creating a repeatable, firm-wide process that ensures
pricing is consistent, fair, and profitable.

Think of pricing as a spectrum:

**•** **Pure hourly billing:** every unit of time is monetized.

**•** **Pure value-based pricing:** time is irrelevant; only the perceived
value matters.

**•** **Hybrid models:** a balance between time-based inputs and
value-oriented factors.

The goal is to combine scope and complexity with client value and internal
business drivers and do it consistently, whether pricing is handled by a
senior partner or an intake specialist.


THE HYBRID PRICING MODEL IN PRACTICE

**Step 1: Establish Project Guidelines**

                              - Set a base fee for each matter type (the minimum you’d accept).

                              - Document the standard inclusions so everyone understands the
baseline scope.

                              - Example: An uncontested divorce might have a base fee that
includes filing, document prep, and one client meeting.

**Step 2: Scope Accurately with Pricing Factors**

                              - Use a checklist to assess complexity and workload.

                              - Examples: number of parties, presence of children, asset
valuations, jurisdictional requirements.

                              - Benefit: Any team member can run the checklist and produce
a consistent scope assessment.

**Step 3: Apply Modifiers (Value-Based Adjustments)**

Modifiers reflect two categories:

                              - Client-Focused Value: urgency, stakes, business impact,
strategic importance.

                              - Internal Firm Drivers: resource constraints, specialized
expertise, alignment with firm goals.


KEY PRICING INSIGHTS FROM ALTFEE

Anonymized and aggregated data from AltFee users highlights clear patterns:

                              - Urgency can increase price by ~5%.

                              - Capacity constraints can justify an ~11% increase.

                              - Adding one or more price-increasing modifiers raises fees by ~10%
on average.


**Step 4: Package the Proposal**

                              - Write a client-facing service description.

                              - Clearly state in scope and out of scope items to prevent disputes.

                              - Include language for common scope changes (e.g., uncontested
matters becoming contested).

                              - Deliver through engagement letters, proposal emails, or integrated
billing software.

**Step 5: Collaborate and Approve**

                              - Intake staff may scope; partners may review; finance may
check margins.

                              - Take learnings and input back into your pricing system so scoping
accuracy improves over time.

                              - Use workflow stages (“Draft → Review → Approve → Propose”) to
keep pricing decisions consistent.

**Step 6: Track, Learn, and Refine**

                              - Compare flat fees to recorded time for every matter.

                              - Adjust base fees and scope descriptions based on

**Why the Framework Works**

                              - Consistency: Clients get uniform pricing regardless of

### **Implementing Flat Fees** **Successfully**


Adopting flat fees doesn’t require overhauling your entire pricing model
overnight. The most successful transitions start small, build confidence,
and expand as the firm gains experience and data.


**Start Small and Build Gradually**

Choose one matter type, practice group, or even a single recurring task as
your pilot. This lets you refine your approach without overwhelming the
team or risking widespread mispricing.

Flat fees work best when you already understand the scope and variability
of the work. Routine, repeatable services are ideal first candidates.


**Use Historical Data as Your Baseline**

If you’ve been recording time, you already have valuable pricing intelligence.
Use past billing records to determine the typical hours and effort for your
chosen pilot work. This historical average becomes the foundation for your
initial flat-fee rate.

Tracking continues to matter even after you switch. Comparing recorded
time to your set fee will show whether you’re protecting your margins or
need to adjust.


**Integrate Pricing Into Intake**

Pricing shouldn’t be an afterthought. Build scoping and fee determination
into your intake workflow so every matter is priced before work begins.
This ensures consistency and prevents uncomfortable mid-matter
pricing conversations.


**Collaborate Across the Team**

Bring in input from those with different perspectives knowing partners,
associates, paralegals, and intake staff all interact with matters differently.
Combining insights leads to more accurate scoping and a pricing process
everyone can trust.

**Treat Every Matter as a Learning Opportunity**

Flat-fee pricing improves with iteration. After each matter, review:

                              - Did actual work match the scoped estimate?

                              - Was the fee profitable at the recorded time and expense level?

                              - Did the client perceive the value as equal to or greater than the fee?

Feed these insights back into your guidelines and checklists so the process
improves over time. The next team member to price that same project type
will benefit from the improved guideline and be able to price with more
accuracy and confidence.


**Shift the Mindset**

Not every flat-fee matter will be a “win” on margin. Some will take longer
than expected; others will deliver strong profitability. The objective is to
refine the system so, on average, your firm scales profitably while delivering
predictable value to clients.


ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

2. Use historical time records to help set your initial price.

3. Make pricing a formal step in intake for all flat-fee matters.

4. Review each completed matter, add any learnings to guidelines,
and adjust pricing factors as needed.

### **Complex & Uncertain Matters**


Not every matter fits neatly into a repeatable package. High-stakes
litigation, multi-party transactions, mergers and acquisitions, and
contentious negotiations often carry a high degree of unpredictability.
Complexity doesn’t have to force a return to hourly billing. It simply
requires a different approach to scoping, pricing, and client
communication. Uncertainty doesn’t mean reverting to hourly billing,
it means designing pricing models that flex with complexity.


**The Challenges of Complex Work**

                              - **Unknown Scope:** Early stages may uncover facts that drastically
change workload.

                              - **Multiple Stakeholders:** More parties mean more communications,
revisions, and delays.

                              - **Unpredictable Timelines:** Regulatory delays, court scheduling,
or negotiation breakdowns can extend matters unexpectedly.

                              - **Variable Client Needs:** Urgency or priorities can shift mid-matter,
changing the scope.


**Stage-Based Pricing for Uncertain Matters**

Stage-based pricing breaks a large matter into smaller, clearly defined
phases, each with its own fixed fee. This gives clients cost certainty one
stage at a time while giving the firm the flexibility to adjust pricing as
new information emerges. It creates transparency, manages risk, and
ensures pricing reflects actual complexity rather than speculation.

Litigation is a strong example: by dividing the case into phases such as
pleadings, discovery, pre-trial motions, and trial, firms can align fees with
the realities of each phase instead of relying on open-ended hourly billing.

PRICING NEGOTIATIONS BY “ROUNDS”

In multi-round negotiations, scope creep is almost guaranteed unless it’s
built into the pricing model.

                               - **Base Fee:** Covers a set number of rounds (e.g., three rounds of
markups and responses).

                               - **Additional Rounds:** Charged at a fixed per-round fee.

                               - **Benefit:** Clients know exactly what extended negotiations will cost,
and the firm is paid for extra effort.


HYBRID MODELS FOR HIGH-RISK/HIGH-REWARD MATTERS

In some cases, a blended structure works best:

                               - A reduced upfront stage fee to cover baseline costs.

                               - A success fee or contingency percentage applied only if a defined
outcome is achieved.

                               - Useful where there’s a measurable “win” but significant upfront work
before results are realized.

COMMUNICATION IS THE SAFETY NET

The strongest defense against scope disputes is proactive,
clear communication:

                               - Define inclusions and exclusions for each stage.

                               - Flag likely scope changes in advance.

                               - Get client approval before entering resource-heavy phases.


ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. Break complex matters into stages, each with its own
deliverables and fixed fee.

2. Limit the included negotiation rounds and price additional
rounds separately.

3. Consider blending stage fees with success fees for suitable
high-stakes work.

4. Build decision points into the process so clients approve
scope and pricing before moving forward.

### **Becoming a Modern Law Firm**


Adopting alternative fee arrangements and a modern pricing framework
is a powerful start, but it’s only the first step. Real transformation requires
building a business culture that rewards efficiency, measures value, and
continually evolves with client expectations and market realities.


THREE PILLARS OF A MODERN LAW FIRM

**1. Client-Centered Service**

                                  - Transparency: Share scope, inclusions, and pricing
rationale upfront.

                                  - Communication: Keep clients informed at key milestones,
especially before stage transitions or when scope might change.

                                  - Feedback Loops: Ask clients after each matter how well
pricing matched their expectations.

**2. Data-Driven Decision Making**

                                  - Track Margins: Compare flat fees to recorded time and
expenses to assess profitability.

                                  - Monitor Win Rates: See which pricing models convert the
most proposals.

                                  - Refine Regularly: Adjust base fees, pricing factors, modifiers,
and scope definitions based on real-world results.

**3. Team Collaboration and Buy-In**

                                  - Shared Knowledge: Develop internal guidelines and
checklists so everyone can price consistently.

                                  - Empower Intake: Train your entire legal team to scope and
price matters confidently within established guidelines.

                                  - Reward Efficiency: Recognize and incentivize process
improvements and value delivery.
