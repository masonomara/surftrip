# **Personal Injury** **Template Guide:** **From Retainer** **to Settlement**


**Personal Injury Template Guide: From Retainer to Settlement**

#### Every personal injury case (and personal injury client) is different. Despite this, most cases follow a similar trajectory.

##### You’ll start by interviewing your new client—where you learn about the accident and its impact on the client’s life to determine whether they have a legitimate case. Next, you’ll sign a retainer agreement to detail the terms of your representation and make things “official.” Depending on the file, you might prepare a demand letter to outline your client’s case and get negotiations going with the defendant and their insurer. Or, you might choose to proceed with depositions of both the plaintiff and defendant before engaging in negotiations. And, when all is said and done—when you reach a settlement with the defendant and hand a check over to your (hopefully happy) client—you'll prepare a settlement sheet making it clear how much the client is receiving after your retainer fee and other expenses have been deducted. Of course, when working through each step—and with a heavy caseload to manage—every minute counts. That’s why we’ve prepared the templates in this guide: to help you make fast work of routine tasks in your personal injury files.

## **Personal injury-specific** **retainer clauses**

The specifics of contingency fee agreements vary between jurisdictions, but the items these
agreements should cover are largely similar. Here are some personal injury and contingency fee
sample clauses that should be considered when drafting your retainer agreement with a client.


**1. WHO IS REPRESENTING THE CLIENT AND IS ELIGIBLE TO SPLIT FEES**

Some jurisdictions require that each participating lawyer or law firm must sign the contract with the
client and must agree to assume joint legal responsibility to the client for the performance of the
services in question as if each were partners of the other lawyer or law firm involved. This is done to
prevent clients having their cases farmed out and non-contributing law firms splitting fees to which
they may not be entitled. A sample clause addressing this concern could read:


This Engagement Agreement is made between

[name of law firm], hereinafter refered to as “Law Firm,” or “We” or “Our” or “Us” and
[name(s) of client(s)], hereinafter referred to as “You”
or “Client(s).” Any fees payable to the Law Firm may be shared between all lawyers directly
employed by Law Firm as per their own policies and agreements. Law Firm and Client agree
that no other law firm or lawyer may be included in this matter absent written notice. Any
third-party lawyer or law firm added following the commencement of the agreement, without
either the discharge or withdrawal of the Law Firm prior, may not share a contingency fee
interest in recovered funds without first a written amendment to this agreement signed by Client,
Law Firm, and the third-party. Furthermore, there are no intended third-party beneficiaries to
the relationship between our law firm and you.

**2. HOW CONTINGENCY FEES ARE CALCULATED**

Regulations may dictate how fees may be charged and calculated in contingency fee cases. Consult
your local regulations and modify this sample clause to clearly communicate to a client how fee
recovery will be calculated. Be sure to explicitly describe if expenses are to be deducted from the
recovery and whether such expenses are to be deducted before or, if not prohibited by statute or
court rule, after the contingent fee is calculated. The writing must clearly notify the client of any
expenses for which the client will be liable regardless of whether the client is the prevailing party.


Client agrees to pay Law Firm’s fee for professional services, based on a contingency fee,
to be charged as follows:

% of the gross amount recovered in a settlement before we have instituted a lawsuit;

% of the gross amount recovered in a settlement after we have instituted a lawsuit;

% of the gross amount recovered after trial has begun;

% of the gross amount recovered if any judgment is appealed, either on your behalf or
by an adverse party, or if garnishment or any proceeding after judgment is necessary to collect
the judgment or any portion of it; and

% of the gross amount recovered if the matter is the subject of a retrial as ordered by
a trial or appellate court.

The percentage recovery will be calculated [before/after] outstanding medical bills, expenses,
and other costs of suit are deducted. [If at the time the case is settled or a judgment is paid,
Client owes the Law Firm for any expenses or other items, Law Firm will deduct that sum from
the gross amount to be paid to Client, with the remaining amount subject to our contingency
fee, as described above.] Law Firm will provide Client with a summary statement listing these
deductions at the time of any payment to them from a settlement or judgment.

Client understands that the rates set forth above, where not set by law, are negotiable between
Law Firm and Client. Client agrees to pay the negotiated, calculated contingency fees based
on the rates and basis set forth above.

**3. HOW YOUR LAW FIRM GETS PAID**

Some administrative law practices see recovered funds being paid directly to their client by default.
This can make recovery of earned contingency fees difficult sometimes. To prevent this, include a
clause in your retainer agreement stating that all recovered funds must be made payable to the law
firm and deposited directly into the law firm’s trust account. Note: you may also need to submit the
appropriate forms with the agency paying the fees.


Client agrees that recovered funds will be first deposited directly into the Law Firm trust account
for fee calculation and expense reimbursement prior to the funds being disbursed to the Client.
Failure to have recovered funds placed in the Law Firm trust account may result in a lien being
filed against the Client for outstanding fees and expenses owed.

Law Firm has a lien on any and all claims that are the subject of Law Firm’s representation under
this Agreement. Law Firm’s lien will be for any sums owing to Law Firm for any unpaid costs,
or attorney’s fees, at the conclusion of Law Firm’s services. The lien will attach to any recovery
Client may obtain, whether by arbitration award, judgment, settlement or otherwise. An effect
of such a lien is that Law Firm may be able to compel payment of fees and costs from any such
funds recovered on behalf of Client even if Law Firm has been discharged before the end of the
case. In the event Law Firm withdraws from representing Client without cause, Law Firm will
not be entitled to any lien for fees. The lien will exist and attach to any recovery only for costs
already advanced by Law Firm


**4. HOW YOU WILL BE COMPENSATED IF THE CLIENT PICKS ANOTHER LAW FIRM**

**AFTER THE CASE HAS BEGUN**

different strategy or approach than your law firm supports. Maybe you need to get some distance
from the client. Provided this separation doesn’t prejudice the client, you may hand off a client’s
file to another law firm. That does not mean you should waive all compensation. Adapt this clause
to retain an interest in any future funds earned that used your contribution in part to achieve.

**6. CLIENT APPROVAL NECESSARY FOR SETTLEMENT**

Clients rely on their lawyers’ expertise when it comes to negotiating and settling personal injury
disputes. Because of this, the client may not realize that they are the final word when it comes to
accepting a settlement offer.

To solve this, approval can be obtained prior to receiving an offer. If the client has agreed to any
amount within a range that includes a current offer on the table, an attorney can accept it on their
behalf. Use this clause to let clients know that acceptable settlement terms still require their express
approval before you can accept on their behalf.


Law Firm will not make any settlement or compromise of any nature of any of Client’s claims
without Client’s prior approval. Client retains the absolute right to accept or reject any settlement.


**7. HOW CLIENTS CAN GET HELP FROM A LAWYER ABOUT THEIR LAWYER**

Some states require specific mandatory language be included in contingency fee agreements between
lawyers and clients.

For example, Florida requires the following language be included in contingency fee agreements
for personal injury or for property damages or for death or loss of services resulting from personal
injuries based on tortious conduct of another, including products liability claims.


The undersigned client has, before signing this contract, received and read the statement
of client’s rights and understands each of the rights set forth in it. The undersigned client has
signed the statement and received a signed copy to refer to while being represented by the
undersigned lawyer(s).

This contract may be canceled by written notification to the lawyer at any time within three
business days of the date the contract was signed, as shown below, and if canceled the client
is not obligated to pay any fees to the attorney for the work performed during that time. If the
lawyer has advanced funds to others in representation of the client, the lawyer is entitled to
be reimbursed for amounts that the lawyer has reasonably advanced on behalf of the client.
