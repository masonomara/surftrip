##### Mid-sized law firms often face the unique challenge of getting their teams aligned around common goals: they’ve outgrown the informal processes that worked as a smaller practice but are not yet resourced like large firms with specialized teams for every challenge. As a result, growth introduces complexity, resource constraints become more pronounced, and managing diverse teams across departments or locations adds further layers of difficulty. At this stage, the ability to set clear, actionable goals is no longer optional— it’s essential for long-term success.


Structured goal-setting frameworks, like OKRs (Objectives and Key
Results) and KPIs (Key Performance Indicators), provide the intentional
strategy needed to thrive in this environment. Unlike vague aspirations
or disconnected metrics, OKRs help firms define ambitious yet measurable
objectives, while KPIs track performance against the most critical indicators
of success. Together, these tools empower firms to focus on what matters most,
align teams around shared priorities, and evaluate progress with precision.


For mid-sized law firms, the stakes are high: scaling effectively requires
clarity, coordination, and accountability. By embracing OKRs and KPIs,
you can bridge the gap between ambition and execution, setting a strong
foundation for sustainable growth while addressing the unique challenges
of your size and stage.

## The building blocks of effective OKRs and KPIs


While OKRs and KPIs are powerful tools for setting and achieving
goals, their effectiveness depends on how well they’re designed
and implemented. Mid-sized law firms often struggle with crafting
effective frameworks, leading to common pitfalls like vague
objectives, irrelevant metrics, or misalignment across teams.
To build a strong foundation for success, it’s crucial to understand
the basic components of OKRs and KPIs and how they can be
tailored to fit the unique needs of your law firm.


**Defining OKRs**

At their core, OKRs consist of two key components:

**• Objective:** A qualitative statement that defines a clear, inspiring,
and overarching goal. This goal should be ambitious yet
achievable, aligning with the firm’s vision and priorities.

**• Key Results:** Quantifiable outcomes that measure progress
toward the objective. Key results should be specific, measurable,
and time-bound, providing a clear indicator of whether the
objective has been successfully met.

For example, a mid-sized law firm might set the following OKRs:

**• Objective:** Enhance client satisfaction and retention.

**• Key Results:**

                    - Increase the firm’s Net Promoter Score (NPS)
by 15% by the end of Q1 2025.

                    - Reduce client response time for inquiries
to under 24 hours by the end of Q1 2025.

                    - Achieve a 20% increase in repeat business
from existing clients within Q1 2025.

In this example, the objective is focused on improving client satisfaction,
while the key results track specific, measurable, and time-bound
outcomes that indicate whether the objective is being achieved.

**Defining KPIs**

KPIs, on the other hand, are metrics used to measure performance
in ongoing processes. Unlike OKRs, which are goal-oriented, KPIs
track the health and efficiency of critical areas within the firm.
These metrics help firms monitor and manage day-to-day activities,
ensuring operations are running smoothly and that long-term goals
are supported.

Examples of KPIs for law firms include:

**• Billable hours per attorney:** A measure of productivity
and time management.

**• Client satisfaction score:** A reflection of service quality
and client experience.

**• New client acquisition rate:** An indicator of growth
and business development efforts.

**• Case resolution time:** A measure of operational
efficiency and client service speed.

While OKRs focus on achieving specific objectives, KPIs provide
a broader view of how well the firm is performing on a daily basis.


**Combining OKRs and KPIs**

While OKRs and KPIs serve different purposes, they are most effective
when used together. OKRs set ambitious, high-level goals, and KPIs
track the performance of ongoing activities that help achieve those
goals. For example, a law firm’s OKR might be to improve operational
efficiency, while the supporting KPIs would measure the key activities
that contribute to that goal.

To effectively reduce case resolution time, it’s essential to establish
a baseline first. Understanding the current average case resolution
time helps set a realistic and measurable key result. This oftenoverlooked step is critical for creating actionable goals that deliver
tangible outcomes. While this guide doesn’t delve deeply into setting
baselines, a simple adjustment—such as specifying the reduction
target from X to Y—can make goals clearer and more achievable.

For example, a mid-sized law firm might set the following OKRs
and related KPIs:

**• Objective:** Improve operational efficiency across the firm.

**• Key Results:**

                    - Reduce case resolution time from X to Y days by the end
of Q1 2025 (e.g., a 15% reduction).

**Creating OKRs and KPIs that work**

Crafting effective OKRs and KPIs is an art that requires intentionality,
alignment, and collaboration. For mid-sized law firms—where growth,
resource management, and team coordination are top priorities—
designing these frameworks with care ensures they drive meaningful
progress without overwhelming your teams.

Here’s how to get it right:


Align your OKRs with your firm’s vision and priorities

For OKRs and KPIs to be impactful, they must connect directly to your
firm’s strategic direction. Goals that don’t align with the big picture
can waste time and resources, creating confusion instead of clarity.

Start by asking: What are our firm’s core priorities for the next
quarter or year? Are we focused on growth, operational efficiency,
client satisfaction, or all three?

Use these answers to create cascading objectives that translate the
firm’s vision into actionable steps at every level.

**• Firm-Wide Objective:** Expand the firm’s footprint in new markets.

**• Practice Group Objective:** Increase the volume of high-value
cases in emerging practice areas.

**• Individual Objective:** Build five new referral partnerships
with complementary firms or organizations.

Cascading objectives ensure that everyone—from partners to associates
to support staff—understands how their efforts contribute to the firm’s
broader success.

Focus on what matters most

Mid-sized firms often face the temptation to set too many goals,
diluting focus and creating confusion. Avoid this by identifying
high-impact objectives that address the firm’s most pressing
challenges or opportunities.

**• Prioritize ruthlessly:** Consider which objectives will make
the biggest difference to the firm’s growth, efficiency, or client
experience.

**• Keep it simple:** Limit firm-wide OKRs to 3–5 quarterly objectives
to maintain focus. Similarly, avoid overloading dashboards
with too many KPIs—focus on those that directly impact
your strategic priorities.

For instance, rather than tracking every conceivable metric,
hone in on KPIs like new client acquisition rate, average case
resolution time, or revenue per lawyer—metrics that provide
actionable insights without overwhelming your team.


Involve your team in the process

Top-down goal-setting often fails to resonate with the people tasked
with executing those goals. Leverage collaboration to create OKRs
and KPIs that engage and motivate teams.

**• Gather input efficiently:** Use brief surveys, brainstorming sessions,
or team workshops to collect ideas and insights. This helps ensure
that objectives and metrics are both realistic and meaningful
to those involved.

**• Foster ownership:** Involve team leaders and practice group heads
in refining OKRs and selecting KPIs. When people see their input
reflected in the final plan, they’re more likely to take ownership
of the outcomes.

For example, a litigation team might highlight the need to reduce
case preparation time. By incorporating this insight into an OKR,
such as “Streamline workflows to reduce average preparation time
by 10%,” you not only set a relevant goal but also empower the
team to contribute to its achievement.

                 - Share the purpose: Explain how OKRs align with the

                  - Address concerns early: Anticipate and address questions

Structure accountability without micromanagement

Accountability is the backbone of any successful OKR and KPI strategy,
but it’s a delicate balance for mid-sized law firms. You need to ensure
progress is tracked and goals are met without creating a culture
of micromanagement that stifles autonomy and trust. By combining
clear ownership, transparent tracking, and effective use of technology,
your firm can foster accountability while empowering your team.


**Create clear ownership**

Every OKR and KPI should have a clear owner responsible for tracking
and driving progress. Assigning ownership ensures accountability
while avoiding confusion about who is responsible for what.

**• Define roles:** Assign OKR and KPI ownership at the appropriate
level. For instance, firm-wide objectives might be owned by
senior leadership, practice group goals by team leads, and
individual goals by associates or staff.

**• Clarify expectations:** Owners aren’t solely responsible for
achieving the goal but are tasked with coordinating efforts,
tracking progress, and reporting updates.

**• Document ownership:** Use a shared document or tool to list
each OKR or KPI, along with its owner and key deadlines,
ensuring everyone knows who to approach for updates.

For example, if a key firm-wide objective is to improve client retention,
the managing partner might own this OKR, with team leads responsible
for sub-objectives like increasing client touchpoints or streamlining
case follow-ups. A key to driving success is creating visibility into
progress, which is critical to fostering accountability without
micromanagement. Transparent tracking lets everyone stay informed
about where things stand, reduces the need for constant check-ins,
and highlights opportunities for support.

Establish a cadence for reviews and updates

Accountability and progress tracking are most effective when
supported by a structured cadence of reviews and updates.
For mid-sized law firms, regular check-ins ensure goals remain
front and center, progress is evaluated, and adjustments are made
as needed—all without disrupting day-to-day operations. By setting
intentional rhythms for reviews, you can keep your team aligned
and your goals on track while maintaining momentum.


**Set check-in intervals**

The frequency of your check-ins should balance the need for
oversight with your firm’s operational tempo.

**• Weekly touchpoints:** Use brief weekly meetings or reports
to review immediate priorities and address short-term
challenges. For instance, practice group leaders might
share updates on case progress or client intake metrics.

**• Monthly reviews:** Schedule more in-depth monthly reviews
to assess broader progress on OKRs and KPIs. This is the
time to identify trends, celebrate wins, and address any
lingering roadblocks.

**• Quarterly evaluations:** Conduct a comprehensive review
of all objectives and key results every quarter. This provides
an opportunity to recalibrate goals, set new priorities,
and align teams with the firm’s evolving strategy.

**Make review meetings effective**

Review meetings are your opportunity to assess progress,

For mid-sized firms juggling multiple priorities, an intentional

By embedding reviews into your firm’s rhythm, you not only

**Analyze results**

Once your team has made progress toward OKRs and KPIs, it’s critical
to assess whether the desired outcomes were achieved. This evaluation
goes beyond simply ticking off completed tasks; it aims to uncover the
deeper impact on your firm’s growth and operations.

**Avoiding common pitfalls in building OKRs and KPIs**

Many mid-sized law firms face challenges when implementing OKRs and KPIs,
often falling into common traps that can undermine their effectiveness. Recognizing
and addressing these pitfalls early on can help your firm build a more structured
and impactful approach to goal-setting:

Setting unrealistic goals

While ambitious objectives can drive growth, setting goals that are too lofty can
demotivate teams. It’s crucial to adjust objectives to be challenging yet achievable,
ensuring they push the firm forward without setting teams up for failure. Break
down large goals into smaller, actionable milestones and be prepared to revisit
goals if they become unrealistic as circumstances change.

Lack of data

Without a clear data-driven approach, it’s difficult to measure success or identify
areas for improvement. Strategies for using available data effectively include
leveraging existing tools and systems to track performance and ensure your KPIs
reflect meaningful metrics. Firms can enhance their data collection by utilizing
legal tech platforms like Clio to automate tracking and generate real-time insights,
minimizing manual work and ensuring accuracy.

Vague objectives

Objectives like “grow the firm” or “improve operations” lack clarity and fail to inspire
action. Objectives should be specific, actionable, and tied to the firm’s overarching
goals. Clear objectives that define the “why” and “how” will guide teams toward
focused action.

Activity-focused key results

Key results that measure tasks (e.g., “send weekly emails to clients”) rather than
outcomes (e.g., “increase client satisfaction by 15%”) fail to provide meaningful
benchmarks for success. Ensure key results focus on outcomes that directly
impact the firm’s success, whether in client acquisition, revenue growth,
or operational efficiency.

Too many KPIs

Overloading dashboards with dozens of metrics dilutes focus and creates unnecessary
complexity. Prioritize a few high-impact KPIs that align with strategic priorities,
ensuring the team is working toward what matters most.

Lack of alignment across teams

One of the biggest challenges in mid-sized firms is ensuring all teams are working
toward the same goals. When objectives aren’t clearly communicated or aligned across
departments, conflicting priorities and inefficiencies can arise. To avoid this, ensure
firm-wide alignment by cascading OKRs from the firm level down to individual teams
and setting up regular check-ins to keep everyone on the same page. This alignment
creates a cohesive effort across departments, maximizing progress toward shared goals.

Failure to review and adjust

Setting and forgetting OKRs or KPIs leads to stagnation. Regular reviews ensure goals
remain relevant and actionable, and adjustments can be made based on performance.
Whether quarterly or monthly, review cycles provide opportunities to pivot and refine
goals based on insights gained.
