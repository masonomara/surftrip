**STEP 1: CONFIGURE YOUR BILLING**

**AUTOMATION SETTINGS**



“This process takes only a few
minutes, and should reflect your
firm’s billing criteria.”



To take full advantage of the billing and collection capabilities available in

Clio, you’ll want to spend a few minutes setting up a few basic parameters

to align with your firm’s standard billing terms. These settings will be the

default for any bills created by your firm, but they can also be customized

for specific users, clients, and even activity types throughout your firm’s

billing cycles—which we’ll explain in more detail in the next section.


Anyone with administrator access can update all of your firm’s settings in

Clio, so you’ll want to ensure the appropriate firm members have these

permissions. This process takes only a few minutes, and should reflect

your firm’s billing criteria.


Once you’ve set up your base settings, you and everyone at your firm can

begin entering their billable line items for individual clients and matters,

paving the way for fast, easy work entries and one-click billing. You’ll also

get the peace of mind that all of these billing parameters are consistent

and accurate in all of the bills you create and send.


Create customized bills in Clio that automatically update key information.

**standard** **billing** **parameters**


Setting your firm’s standard billing parameters will ensure that all of your

bills reflect your everyday operations:


**• Currency.** Clio defaults to $USD as the primary currency,

but you can customize to other currencies (€, £, ¥, etc.) based

on your location.


**• Tax rates.** If applicable, you can set one or more tax rates

according to how you want them to be applied to a client’s bill.


**• Time rounding increments.** Clio gives you the option to round time

entries to a specific decimal amount or custom minute increment,

as determined by your firm’s common practice.


**• Terms of bill payment.** You can set custom terms for payment,

which include grace day periods, discounts for early payments,

or interest amounts to be charged when the bill goes unpaid

beyond a set number of days. Different terms can be created for

different client types.

**default** **billing** **rates** **and** **fees**


Be sure to set your firm’s baseline billing rates and fees, which can

include multiple parameters for different types of matters and activities.

These rates and fees will be the baseline for calculating bill totals, which

can be adjusted at any time leading up to the final issuing of the bill:


**• Hourly rates.** Each member at your firm can set their own billing

rate, which will apply automatically to any of their time entries

logged in Clio. For firms that charge unique rates for different

clients or case types, these amounts can be customized for each

matter or contact.


**• Flat fee activity types.** For firms charging flat rates, you can preset

fees based on individual billable tasks to reflect your firm’s rate

sheet. Logging those tasks in your billing software will automatically

attribute those base fees, which can also be customized for each

client, matter, or individual task.


**• Contingency fee rate.** Contingency fee rates can be set for each

matter, which will determine how much is owed to your firm when

a bill is generated with a settlement amount. For all contingency
based matters, you can also log expense items to be billed as their

own line items on an invoice.


For all flat fee activities and contingency fee matters, you can also log

non-billable time to better understand productivity and profitability for

this work.

**STEP 2: LOG WORK INTO CLIO**



“One way to maximize the benefit
of Clio’s billing workflows is to

their work directly into Clio.”



Without a system like Clio, inputting all of your firm’s work into individual

bills is a time-consuming and arduous task to take on every month.


Whether you’ve been inputting this data from a paper time sheet or a

meticulous spreadsheet, it takes time to recreate all of this information

within each bill, and then to reference and include all of the unique

contact information stored in your firm’s address book. In other words,

even if you log all of your time regularly, not doing it directly in your

billing software means you’ll have to do it more than once.


Clio provides a comprehensive system to help you and everyone at your

firm manage their time entries, ensuring that all records are linked to a

specific matter and contact—making it easier and more convenient for

Clio to assemble bills in bulk at the end of the month.







**record** **work** **entries**


One way to maximize the benefit of Clio’s billing workflows is to have

individual contributors log their work directly into Clio.


Whether logging time entries or flat fee activities, inputting this

information directly into Clio saves the lawyer, paralegal, or assistant

from having to do it later on. It’s also a great way to ensure those billable

work entries are logged in a secure place where they won’t be lost or

forgotten before it comes time to send out the associated bills.

unique contact information, issue date, work entries, task descriptions,

calculated tax amounts, and bill totals. Each invoice will also be assigned

its own due date and invoice number.


Clio will identify all billable clients and allow you to create a list of draft

bills by clicking New Bills.

**STEP 4: REVIEW BILLS**



“All of your billing information
will always be up to date in all
places at all times.”



Once your bills have been created, they can be submitted to the

responsible attorneys for review. All reviewing attorneys will receive a

notification email with a custom message and link that directs them to a

list of “pending invoices” for review. If you’re doing this on your own, you

can skip this step—but it’s also a valuable opportunity to review all of

your line items to ensure nothing was missed.


Each attorney can quickly scroll through each bill, approving them one at

a time or in bulk. They can also edit time entries, add more time, or add

or revise the associated notes and dates. All updates made to draft bills

will be reflected in the associated time entries and descriptions entered

for their associated matters and clients. This means that all of your

billing information will always be up to date in all places at all times.


Edit time entries, add more time, or add or revise notes and dates any

time before sending a bill to a client.


**add** **discounts, credit** **notes, and** **write-offs**


Since the billing attorney will know the most about their client’s casework,

billing situation, and overall relationship with the firm, they’re able to apply

discounts as needed while reviewing bills. They can apply discounts to

individual line items or bill totals. Credit notes and write-off amounts can

also be applied later on if needed.

**apply** **funds** **held** **in** **trust**


As managers of client funds held in trust, attorneys can apply trust

These trust payments will be clearly documented on the client bills,

Applying trust amounts to bill totals can also be done in bulk.

This function will apply available trust amounts to any outstanding

bill totals, while also calculating totals for any amounts outstanding.

**STEP 5: SHARE BILLS WITH CLIENTS**


Without a system like Clio, once your bills have been created, sharing

them takes a significant amount of time if you’re sending them to each

contact individually (either by mail or electronically via email). Clio’s

billing and collection workflows are designed to eliminate the work it

takes to share bills with clients individually by doing this for you in one

bulk action.


In Clio, when you store email information within each client’s billing

profile, sharing bills is easy. Once bills have been approved, they can be

shared in bulk or individually via email or secure client portal—complete

with customized messaging.


**• Sharing by email.** Clients will receive your message with a link that

allows them to view or download their bill.


**• Sharing by client portal.** Clients will receive a link to a password
protected portal, which they can log into to view their bill.


Billing clients electronically also gives you the advantage of being able

to collect payments electronically, saving you from having to manually

update your billing records and submit a check to the bank yourself

(more on this below). For clients who prefer to receive hard copies of

their invoices, you can also print select invoices individually or in bulk.


Quickly share multiple bills via email or secure client portal with the

click of a button.

**STEP 6: COLLECT PAYMENTS**



“57% of electronic payments get
paid within 24 hours of receiving
a bill.”



In the full scheme of everything, billing clients is really only the beginning

of what you’re really trying to accomplish: getting paid.


There are multiple ways for clients to pay their bills, and Clio’s billing

and collection workflows are designed to reflect this. Whether clients are

paying by check, by funds held in trust, or electronically via credit card,

Clio will help ensure that all of your revenue-generating records are up

to date at all times.


**electronic** **payments**


By far, the easiest, most convenient way for both clients and lawyers to

settle a bill is through electronic payments. While paper billing requires

printing, envelopes, and physically mailing bills to clients, electronic bills

are instantaneous and efficient.


Clio’s billing and collection workflows ensure that you have a full record

of all your past billing as well. With electronic payments, Clio will update

your billing records—making day-to-day bookkeeping that much easier

and more efficient


Here’s what a typical payment workflow looks like through Clio:


1. The client receives their bill immediately with a secure payment link

to pay their bill.

2. The client clicks the payment link and submits their payment

information, much like they would using PayPal or any other secure

payment service.

3. Once the payment has been completed, Clio updates the bill status

to “Paid,” and the funds are automatically transferred to your firm’s

bank account.

**STEP 7: FOLLOW UP ON**

**UNPAID BILLS**



“Clio makes it easy to see which
bills haven’t been paid, how
long they’ve been outstanding,
and what amounts are due.”



Having a dedicated billing and payment collection system in place

helps you keep all of your firm’s billing data in one place. This makes it

incredibly more efficient to assemble all of your bills every month. It also

makes it much easier to manage all of your historical billing information

when you need quick access.


**better** **billing** **systems** **make** **for** **better** **billing** **management**


When dealing with clients who don’t pay on time—whether it’s just once or

on a regular basis—following up can be inconvenient and time-consuming

without the right system in place. And, often, it’s not because they don’t

want to pay you, they just forget. This means—like it or not—it’s up to you

to remind them.


Part of the challenge is identifying which bills haven’t been paid—but the

work involved in reaching out to each delinquent client with details of

what they owe can be just as challenging.


Clio makes it easy to see which bills haven’t been paid, how long they’ve

been outstanding, and what amounts are due. You can quickly sort and

filter outstanding bills based on custom information, including unique

client attributes or issue date, and quickly send email reminders to

clients. The reminders will include all outstanding invoices, total amount

outstanding, a link to pay their bill online, and an accompanying message

that can easily be customized. Bills can have multiple recipients, and

once designated as a recipient, they’ll automatically appear when sharing

future bills.

When you track all of your firm’s billing and payment information in one

place, Clio can generate detailed reports on some of your firm’s most
