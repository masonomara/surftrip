##### **CHAPTER 1**
## **Marketing for Solo Lawyers**

For solo practitioners, effective marketing is essential to survival and the
key to success and sustainable growth. While larger law firms can often
count on brand recognition and referral networks to help bring in clients,
solo practitioners must find ways to stand out on their own in a highly
competitive, increasingly digital legal market.

In this first chapter, we’ll explore how you can use modern marketing
strategies and digital tools to define your solo firm’s brand, attract more
potential clients, and grow your practice. We will break down the most
effective marketing channels for solo lawyers, including email marketing,
social media, Local Services Ads (LSA), search engine marketing (SEM),
and search engine optimization (SEO).

The chapter also offers practical tips for tracking the return on investment
(ROI) of your marketing efforts and highlights how attribution can help
identify your most effective strategies.


**WHY MARKETING IS IMPORTANT FOR SOLO LAWYERS—**
**AND HOW IT’S CHANGING**

Effectively marketing your firm to attract new clients while retaining
existing ones is especially challenging for solo practitioners with limited
time and resources. This is why it’s so crucial for solo firms to be strategic
with their marketing efforts.

One of the most significant pieces in the marketing puzzle for solo firms
involves recognizing how shifts in client behavior and technology have
changed the landscape of legal marketing. Law firm marketing in years
past centered on traditional channels like print ads, TV and radio ads,
and direct mail. Today, effective legal marketing increasingly focuses
on reaching potential clients through digital strategies like SEO and
social media.

Fortunately for solo firms—with the right tools—digital marketing
strategies can be more cost-effective, time-efficient, and targeted than
traditional ads. By marketing to potential clients in the places they’re
actively searching, you can attract more clients with less wasted effort.

**SEARCH ENGINE MARKETING (SEM)**

Another way to get your firm noticed online in search engine results is
by running targeted paid ads, such as Google Ads, to appear in search
results. With SEM, you run ads that appear in search results when
people search for certain keywords. You only pay when someone clicks
on your ad—it’s pay-per-click (PPC) marketing.

Using SEM like Google Ads can be effective because Google is the
primary place where people search online for lawyers. But, this type of
marketing with a broad reach can also be expensive, depending on your
firm’s location and practice area. That’s where Google’s Local Services
Ads can help.


**LOCAL SERVICES ADS (LSA)**

To get the most out of your marketing dollars as a solo practitioner,
it’s wise to be targeted with online advertising. Enter: Local Services
Ads (LSA), which can help you drive local leads.

LSAs are pay-per-lead ads via Google that appear at the top of
search engine results for searchers nearby. When using LSAs for
your solo firm, you only pay when a potential lead calls or messages
you specifically from the ad. In this way, LSAs match potential
customers searching for a specific service (such as your legal services)
with relevant businesses in the same area (like your firm), and the
business (i.e., you) only pays if the customer reaches out. This means
more reliable suggestions for potential clients, more qualified leads,
and a better return on your advertising spend.

**HOW TO TRACK THE ROI OF YOUR MARKETING DOLLARS**

In addition to using attribution to learn which marketing efforts
are bringing clients to your firm, you’ll also want to track the return
on investment (ROI) of your marketing dollars to ensure your budget
is optimized.

Put simply, this means having a strategy for quantifying how much
you spend on each marketing channel you use, and what financial
return you receive in turn. Knowing which marketing activities bring
you the highest return helps you better focus your efforts and budget
in the future.

Here are a few simple ways to measure how well your marketing dollars
are working:

  - Do a deep dive into your website’s analytics. By providing
information about where website traffic is coming from, a website
analytics tool like Google Analytics can be used to measure ROI
by assessing how much traffic and how many new clients come
to your firm from things like paid ads. Using website analytics,
you can also track what website a person was visiting before
they visited your firm’s site (i.e., the referring domain, such as a
social media post or an external website link), which can help you
determine ROI for specific digital marketing efforts.

  - Use UTM parameters on marketing links. When you add UTM
parameters to the URLs you use for specific marketing campaigns
(such as the link in a social media post), you can use them to track
that link’s performance. This lets you then track performance
and calculate ROI for specific campaigns.

  - Track paid ad performance. Similarly, if you’re using paid ads
(like Google Ads or social media ads), you can look at their metrics
to determine how many conversions the paid ads translated to,
such as intake form submissions, and compare that to how much
you paid for the ad that brought them there.

## **Intake for Solo Lawyers**

Your marketing efforts were successful, and potential clients have found
your firm. Next, you want to make a strong first impression and give those
new clients a seamless client experience.

This chapter will cover how solo lawyers can make the most of the critical
process of client intake. We’ll outline best practices for capturing client

We will also explore various channels to simplify the intake process for

**BEST PRACTICES FOR CAPTURING CLIENT DATA**

Collecting essential information from potential clients is a crucial part
of the client intake process. Consider the following best practices to
streamline the process as a solo lawyer:

  - Have a structured intake process. Create a standardized intake
process that you can apply to each potential client, including
standard intake forms that ask for all necessary client information
such as contact information, relevant legal history, and details of
their case. This helps ensure that nothing gets missed at the outset.

  - Use online intake forms. Providing clients with secure online
intake forms that link to your case management system saves time,
streamlines data entry, and makes it easier and more convenient for
clients to share their information. This is key for solo practitioners
who want to provide a smooth and professional onboarding
experience for clients—but can’t be everywhere at once.

  - Include pre-screening questions. By asking targeted pre-screening
questions when collecting initial client information, you can quickly
determine if a client relationship is a good fit or if there’s an obvious
conflict—saving you and the potential client time.
