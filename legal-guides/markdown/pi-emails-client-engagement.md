Email Templates for Personal Injury Firms: Effective Client Engagement

##### With personal injury cases, timely responses are key to earning trust and retaining clients, and having prepared templates on hand can help accelerate your communications. But it’s not enough to be quick. It’s important that clients know that you understand their problems, that your communications are informative, and that you are able to demonstrate approachability and care.

###### Your initial engagements typically encompass more than that first touch. You’ll want to ensure you’re equipped to give all the necessary information your clients need and that you’re able to help them navigate the process of working with your firm.



_Tip:_



To make the best use of the templates in this guide, make sure to review
them and tailor them based on the services your firm provides. Based on the
situation, you may also want to combine messaging from multiple templates
into a separate communication.


Remember that these templates are provided for educational purposes only.
Lawyers should always use professional judgment when using or revising these
templates, which may require amendments based on your jurisdiction and the
needs of your particular file.

###### **EMAIL #1**
### **Introductions with a request** **to book a consultation**

When clients first reach out, you’ll want to follow up with a personal note and an invitation
to book a consultation. With this communication, you will want to demonstrate that you’re
able to look more closely at their situation, that you are prepared to deal with a range of
scenarios related to their problem, and that you understand their cares and concerns.

Use this template to establish a personal connection with the client, showcase your law
firm’s expertise, and invite conversation to discuss the specifics of the client’s situation.
You can also use this email as a follow-up for clients that have submitted any initial
pre-screening information.


Subject: Your personal injury case: How we can assist you


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


Thank you for contacting [LAW FIRM NAME] regarding your personal injury case.
We understand how challenging this time can be, and we’re here to help guide you
through the legal process.


At [LAW FIRM NAME], we have a proven track record of representing clients in
personal injury cases related to [INSERT CASE TYPES].


To begin, we would like to gather some preliminary details about your case. Could you
kindly provide a brief overview of the accident and any injuries you have sustained?

[ADD ADDITIONAL INFORMATION REQUESTED or LINK PRE-SCREENING INTAKE FORM]


Once we review your information, we will schedule a consultation to discuss your
legal options.


If you have any questions, feel free to reach out to us directly.


We look forward to working with you.


Best regards,

###### **EMAIL #2**
### **Initial follow-up with prescreening**

If your law firm deals with a high volume of inquiries, you may need to have a system
in place for prescreening potential clients and identifying referrals from past clients.
This saves you the time of following up with every potential client, many of whom may
not be a fit for your services.

An initial prescreening email should be sent promptly when a client first reaches out,
as this will help encourage them to follow up while the problem is top of mind. By providing
a list of case types in your response, you can also help potential clients self-assess whether
your firm is the right fit for them. The following template can be used to solicit brief details
of their situation via an email response, or if you have an online intake form for
prescreening your inquiries, this can be used instead.


Subject: Your personal injury case: Thank you for reaching out

###### **EMAIL #3**
### **Consultation follow-up with** **engagement request**

Once you’ve had a consultation and the client has decided to retain your services,
this template can be used to share documents that you require to be filled out, as well
as any other information relevant to your client.

The sooner this email is sent out to the client after meeting with them, the more
likely they will be to fill out the information you need in a timely manner.


Subject: Welcome to [LAW FIRM NAME] - Your case onboarding


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


Thank you for choosing [LAW FIRM NAME] to assist you with your personal injury case.
We are committed to providing you with dedicated and professional service every step of the
way.


To begin, please find attached the necessary consent forms and the engagement agreement,
which outlines our relationship, the services we will provide, and the terms of our
representation. Kindly review and sign these documents at your earliest convenience.


Next steps:


1. Review and sign the attached engagement agreement.


2. Complete the consent forms, which will allow us to begin the necessary investigations

into your case.

3. Once these forms are submitted, we’ll follow up with the information requests that we
require.


If you have any questions or need assistance with any of the forms, please do not hesitate to
reach out.


We look forward to working with you and helping you through this process.


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]


[LAW FIRM NAME]

###### **EMAIL #4**
### **Consultation follow with** **undecided client**

Sometimes, after an initial consultation with a potential client, they may be unsure
or undecided on whether they want to proceed with hiring you and your law firm. This
template can be used to follow up with these clients to assure them that you are ready
and available to support them and to let them know that all they need to do is reach out.

Even if they don’t pursue their legal matter with you, it’s good to leave them with a note
of record that you discussed their matter and to leave them with a positive impression of
what it’s like to work with your firm.


Subject: Thank you for your consultation with [LAW FIRM NAME]


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


Thank you for taking the time to meet with us regarding your personal injury case.
We appreciate the opportunity to learn more about your situation, and we want to
ensure you have all the information you need moving forward.


In the meantime, please feel free to reach out if you have any additional questions or need
clarification on any part of the process.


If you would like to proceed with representation, please let us know, and we will send over the
necessary engagement agreement and consent forms.


We look forward to assisting you in seeking justice and fair compensation.


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]


[LAW FIRM NAME]


[PHONE NUMBER] | [EMAIL ADDRESS]

###### **EMAIL #5**
### **Legal fees and costs estimate**

For many clients, having a general sense of what it will cost to pursue their legal matter
will factor into their decision on whether to move forward with it and which law firm
they choose to hire. Being upfront with your rates and how contingency fees work for
their case shows transparency and reinforces a sense of trust with potential clients.

As the question of cost may come up in conjunction with other questions throughout the
intake process, the contents of this email can be included within your other follow-up
communications as well.


Subject: Estimated legal fees and costs for your personal injury case


Dear [CLIENT NAME],


We understand that managing legal fees and costs is an important consideration. Our fees for
handling your personal injury case would be a [PERCENTAGE] contingency fee, not including
additional costs that may be required.


We are committed to transparency throughout the process, and we want you to feel confident
in our approach. If you have any questions about how these fees would be applied, or about
anything else regarding your case, please don’t hesitate to ask.


Thank you for your trust in [LAW FIRM NAME].


Best regards,


[YOUR NAME] | [YOUR POSITION]


[LAW FIRM NAME]


[PHONE NUMBER] | [EMAIL ADDRESS]
